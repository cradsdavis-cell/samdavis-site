The honest answer

Most AI initiatives stall in the same five places.

  • The reporting feature your accountants wanted is half-deployed.
  • Each new client still needs a week of taxonomy mapping.
  • The chat assistant your sales team built doesn't sound like you.
  • Your engineers are split across three roadmaps.
  • You hired an agency once. Six figures, three months, nothing shipped.

The bottleneck isn't the model. It's everything around it.

What I keep seeing

Five patterns I keep finding in the work I've done.

1
The integration trap.
The model works in a notebook. Then it has to read your customer's data format, write to your audit-trail, route to your CRM, and explain itself when it's wrong. That's where projects stall.
2
The adoption gap.
Features ship into a vacuum because the team that has to use them — sales, customer success, the channel partner — wasn't in the room when they were being built.
3
The hand-tuning death spiral.
Every new client takes a week of taxonomy work, supplier categorisation, or chart-of-accounts wrangling. Growth caps at the rate you can hire analysts. AI applied right turns this into a one-hour configuration.
4
The architecture trap.
AI buried inside a process tends to fail silently and break the audit trail. AI sitting above the process — designing and adapting it — keeps the audit trail clean and the failure modes legible.
5
The translation gap.
Between technical and non-technical, between platform team and customer team, between what the LLM can do and what your product needs it to do. That's the actual job.
