How it works

We don't build it for you. We teach you to build it.

No deck, no theatre. Here's the real shape of a Coaching Block — four sessions where you stand up your own AI executive assistant, and the work you do between them that makes it stick.

The 4-session arc

Most people come in through a Coaching Block — four 1:1 sessions over about four weeks, with real building of your own between each one. The sessions teach the method; the between-session work is where it lands.

Session 1 · with Sam

Get Claude Code running, build a sandbox

We finish whatever's left of the install, get you comfortable in Claude Code, and fire your first prompts together — the Karpathy + Nate Herk starter prompts from Pack 2. We set up Obsidian as your reading window and build a sandbox: a safe space you can poke at without breaking anything.

You leave with a working setup and somewhere to play.

Between 1 and 2 — you play in the sandbox. Have ten real conversations with it. The goal isn't to get anything right — it's to stop being nervous about the tool.Pack 2

Session 2 · with Sam

Introduce Pack 3, start the flower exercise

Now that you're comfortable, we open up Pack 3 properly and start restructuring your setup into a real executive assistant. We begin the flower exercise — a structured way of mapping who you are, what you do, and what matters — so the assistant has a real model of you to work from. You also walk me through your world, so I understand the business we're building this around.

Between 2 and 3 — you finish the flower exercise and work through the rest of Pack 3 at your own pace. Slack's open for questions.Pack 3

Session 3 · with Sam

Connect your tools

We wire the assistant into the tools you actually live in — Google Workspace, or whatever your equivalent is (Gmail, Calendar, Sheets and more). Now it can read and act, not just talk. Then I push you to keep exploring and experimenting on your own; this is where most people find the workflow that changes their week.

Between 3 and 4 — you work through Pack 4 — deeper connections, your network layer, and the more advanced moves.Pack 4

Session 4 · with Sam

Wrap up, connect more, go solo

We review what's running, wire up any further MCP connections you want, and map what you'd build next. The point of this session is confidence: you leave knowing how to keep growing the system yourself, without me.

If you'd rather keep a hand on your shoulder, the Continuation Retainer is there — but most people don't need it. That's the goal.

Training wheels, then the open road

Think of it like teaching someone to ride a bike. Early on we hold the seat and give clear, explicit instructions — every prompt spelled out, nothing assumed. As you get steadier, we let go a little, build trust with the system, and hand it more to do. By Session 4 you're riding on your own and choosing where to go next — and the bike's yours.

Everything you get

It's not just the four calls. Your materials unlock in your private dashboard on this site as you move through the arc — so you're never handed everything at once, only the next piece when you're ready for it.

That's the point of teaching you to build it: it keeps growing after I step back.
