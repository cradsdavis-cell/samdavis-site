Q1 — Which register do you actually mean?

"Multinational consulting" can mean very different visual languages. Pick the one closest to what you mean — or tell me in terminal it's none of these.

A

Big consulting — corporate institutional

Reference: mckinsey.com, bcg.com, bain.com, deloitte.com, accenture.com

Visual language: deep navy + white + single accent (red/orange/green per firm). Heavy editorial photography of business people in real offices. Insight-led content as the centre. Big serif headlines + clean sans body. Article cards. "Trusted by Fortune 500" register. Lots of content.

What it signals: institutional weight, decades of credibility, scale.

Risk for you: looks LARP-y on a solo operator site. Impact founders often distrust this aesthetic ("MBB pretender"). Hard to honour stay-within-ability-level.

B

Strategy boutique — sharp confident editorial

Reference: sequoiacap.com, a16z.com, indexventures.com, anthropic.com (company), stripe.press

Visual language: generous whitespace + distinctive serif display + clean sans body. Restrained palette (often one strong neutral + one accent). Editorial photography but used sparingly. Long-form essays as the centre. Confident copy that doesn't oversell. Often single-column reading column with wide margins.

What it signals: "we think deeply, we have taste, we don't need to shout."

Fit for you: Highest. Matches your voice (concrete/vulnerable/contrast-framed) AND signals premium without LARPing scale.

C

Founder-tools premium — design-forward technical

Reference: linear.app, vercel.com, stripe.com, framer.com, anthropic.com (product)

Visual language: sharp geometric sans (Inter/Geist/SF Pro). Strong dark mode default or duo-tone. Product screenshots as hero. Heavy use of motion + micro-interactions. Technical-precise copy. "Built for builders" register.

What it signals: technical credibility, product-thinking, "I ship."

Risk for you: register signals "AI product startup" more than "AI advisor to founders." Could work but pushes you toward product-shape positioning.

D

Plain-text-but-deep — personal/intellectual

Reference: patrickcollison.com, paulgraham.com, naval.com, marginalrevolution.com, slatestarcodex (legacy)

Visual language: almost no design at all. Black text on cream/white. One serif. No images. No CTAs. Long-form essays. Books read recently. Lists of curiosities.

What it signals: "the work speaks. I don't need to look like anything."

Risk for you: wrong for a commercial-conversion surface. Right for a personal site behind it. Worth considering as a separate /sam page.
